Carbon Fiber Composite Structures: Strength, Failure, and the Space Between

Researching how CF and hybrid composites behave under bending loads — and building the molds and specimens to test it properly.

Carbon Fiber Vacuum Infusion CNC Machining 3-Point Bending Mechanical Testing Composites Manufacturing
🧪

Carbon fiber is often treated as a magic material — light, stiff, strong. The reality is more complicated. A poorly designed CF layup can fail catastrophically at loads well below what the datasheet suggests, and hybrid composites behave in ways that aren't captured by rule-of-mixtures predictions alone. This research set out to measure that gap directly.

Specimen Design

The study focused on two geometries: I-beams and arched beams. I-beams isolate bending behavior cleanly — the flanges carry tension and compression, the web handles shear. Arched beams introduce curvature effects and interlaminar stress concentrations that flat specimens miss.
