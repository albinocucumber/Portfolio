About Me

Hardware that has to survive the real world

I'm a mechanical engineering student at USC's Viterbi School of Engineering, graduating May 2026. My work sits at the intersection of mechanical design and manufacturing — which means I spend a lot of time thinking about tolerances, fixturing, and what happens when a part actually has to be made at scale, not just look good in CAD.

At Multiply Labs, I redesigned robotic cartridges and docking hardware for medical GMP environments. Before that, I spent two years leading chassis engineering on USC's Solar Car and researching composite structures at USC's Advanced Composites Research Lab. I've machined hundreds of parts, run FEA on more chassis configurations than I can count, and spent enough time in makerspaces to troubleshoot pretty much any fabrication problem.

Outside of engineering, I play guitar and stay active. I'm fluent in English and Mandarin Chinese (Seal of Biliteracy).
